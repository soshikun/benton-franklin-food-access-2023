About Us & Map

Welcome to the Food Access 2023 interactive web map! This map is designed to provide valuable insights into the accessibility of grocery stores, convenience stores, and produce-selling convenience stores within the borders of Benton and Franklin counties. By examining various distances and means of transportation, we aim to shed light on the relative availability of food resources in these areas.

The primary objective of this web map is to assist individuals, policymakers, and organizations in understanding the food access landscape of Benton and Franklin counties. Access to fresh and healthy food is a crucial factor in promoting community well-being and addressing food security concerns.

Data for this map is sourced from the Benton-Franklin Health District and the US Census. These reputable sources ensure that the information presented is reliable and accurate. We extend our gratitude to both organizations for providing the necessary data to create this map.

Using this interactive web map, you can explore and analyze the distribution of grocery stores, convenience stores, and produce-selling convenience stores within the two counties. The map provides different distance thresholds and transportation modes, allowing you to assess the availability of food resources according to your specific needs or research interests.

Thank you for using the Food Access 2023 web map. We hope that it serves as a valuable resource in understanding and addressing food access challenges in Benton and Franklin counties.
